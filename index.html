✦ About This Paradise

The Island
They Sell You.
And the One That Exists.

Antigua is beautiful. That is not in dispute. It is an island of breathtaking beaches — one for every day of the year, so they say. The water is turquoise. The sun is generous. The resort staff smile with professional warmth. * Smiling is a condition of employment. The wages do not always inspire it naturally.

What this advertisement — like all advertisements — carefully omits is the texture of daily life for the 100,000 people who do not leave when the vacation ends. The roads that do not reach the resorts. The hospital with aging equipment. The library damaged by a 1974 earthquake and never rebuilt. Tourism generated the funds. The library remained closed. * A new airport terminal was constructed. Priorities are a form of honesty, if you look closely enough.

"That the native does not like the tourist is not hard to explain. For every native of every place is a potential tourist, and every tourist is a native of somewhere... but some natives — most natives in the world — cannot go anywhere."
— Adapted from Jamaica Kincaid, A Small Place
